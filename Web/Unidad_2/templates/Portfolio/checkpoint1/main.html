Volatility with Regimes

This chart shows the temporal evolution of realized volatility classified into three regimes: Low, Medium, and High. Thresholds are calculated using the 33rd and 67th percentiles, allowing identification of market stress periods and structural changes in asset behavior.

Autocorrelation Function (ACF)

The Autocorrelation Function (ACF) measures volatility persistence over time. Bars exceeding the gray zone (95% confidence intervals) indicate significant autocorrelation. High ACF in early lags suggests volatility clustering.

Absolute Returns vs Rolling Volatility

This visualization overlays absolute returns (bars) with rolling volatility (red line), showing how sharp price movements relate to increases in measured volatility.

Volatility vs Trading Volume

The scatter plot examines the relationship between trading volume and volatility. The correlation coefficient (ρ) quantifies this relationship: positive values indicate that higher trading activity is associated with higher volatility.
